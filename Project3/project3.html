About Me

Hello, my name is Mallika Karran. I am 17 years old and currently a senior at Martin Van Buren High School. After I graduate, I plan to go to college. In college, I want to major in digital art and design. Why digital art and design? Because I can produce digital art to offer more knowledge about the environment, politics, and identity. Creating digital art can demonstrate the connections between these problems and society. Increasing public knowledge of ecological issues is another way that digital art may support environmental sustainability. I can put my talent to use by making impactful pictures and videos that draw attention to the problems that our world faces, such as pollution, animal extinction, climate change, and deforestation. I can also use my skills to make creations to advocate for social change and voice their political opinions. Furthermore, digital art can help me explore my creativity. Each artwork I make can reflect a time period in my life. And other artists can see the development of my identity through time. Identity formation and preservation on a personal and collective level are made possible by art. Art can help me illuminate historical occurrences, cultural values, foreign cultural influences, the endurance of certain customs, and the resistance and modification of particular customs. Through my art, I can connect with other artists and express a common identity, in addition to expressing my uniqueness.
